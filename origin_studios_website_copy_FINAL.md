**Section label:** `Why most websites fail in 2026`

**Headline:**
> Your Traffic Isn't Disappearing.
> It's Being Redirected. To Your Competitors.

**Intro paragraph:**
The analytics dip isn't a mystery. It's a predictable outcome of building for 2020 SEO in a 2026 AI search landscape. Google didn't update its algorithm. It replaced the entire model — and most agencies haven't told their clients yet.

---

**01 — AI Overviews ate 58% of organic clicks. And they're still eating.**
`Source: Ahrefs, Feb 2026`

AI Overviews now appear on 72% of queries. The answer appears. The user never clicks. Your rankings didn't drop. Your clicks did. Because ranking #1 on Google now means being the fourth result below an AI that already answered the question.

**02 — Pure AI content is a countdown timer, not a strategy.**
`Source: SE Ranking, 2025`

SE Ranking tracked 20 websites built entirely on AI-generated content. After Google's spam update targeting "scaled content abuse" — not some sites, not most sites — all twenty lost every keyword ranking. Every AI-built site is one update away from the same outcome.

**03 — Builder sites are invisible to every AI engine.**
`Source: Entity & Schema Gap`

Wix, Framer, and Durable produce zero structured JSON-LD data. No Knowledge Graph signals. No entity relationships. AI engines don't cite what they can't understand in machine-readable form. If your site doesn't speak structured data, it doesn't exist to ChatGPT.

**04 — You're missing the highest-converting channel that now exists.**
`Source: Conversion data, 2026`

AI search converts at 14.2%. Traditional organic: 2.8%. That's a 5x gap. Every month your business is invisible to AI engines is a month your competitors are taking customers you never knew you lost.

**05 — In India, the category leaders haven't been established yet.**

GEO and AEO are still new enough in the Indian market that almost no business has built a dominant AI citation position in any category. The window is open right now. In 12 months, it will start closing. The businesses that adapt now will compound an advantage the latecomers can't buy their way into.

## Edge 02 — Motion Design & 3D as Standard

**Card headline:**
> The web has two kinds of sites: ones people scroll past, and ones people screenshot.

**Body:**
Static is invisible. Every site in every category looks roughly the same: hero image, headline, three feature columns, testimonials, footer. Your prospect has seen it a thousand times. They scroll. They leave.

Motion changes this — not because animation is impressive, but because it signals craft. It tells the user: someone cared. And in a world where every competitor can generate a site in 10 minutes with an AI builder, craft is the one differentiator that can't be faked or commoditised.

We build three layers of motion — and all three are performance-optimised, purpose-designed, and included as standard. Not farmed to a freelancer. Not charged as an expensive add-on. Built in-house, in-brief, from the start.

**What this includes:**

*Lottie Animations*
Vector animations exported from After Effects. Loading states, icon animations, scroll-triggered reveals, illustrated hero sections. Average file size: under 80KB. Performance impact: negligible. Visual impact: dramatic.

*Three.js 3D Environments*
Interactive 3D scenes built directly in the browser. Product showcases that respond to cursor movement. Particle systems that turn a hero section into something genuinely memorable. Abstract environments driven by scroll position. Built to run at 60fps without affecting your Lighthouse score.

*Micro-Interaction Systems*
A complete interaction language for the entire site — hover states, button animations, form transitions, page-change effects. Designed in Figma. Built in code. The difference between a site that feels alive and one that feels assembled.

**The result:**
> Your competitor has a nice website.
> You'll have an experience they don't know how to build.
